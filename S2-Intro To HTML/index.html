German Shepherd

German Shepherds are famous for their intelligent, loyal, and hardworking natures. They are often used as guide, therapy,search-and-rescue, police, military, and narcotics and explosives detecting dogs. While they can come across as aloof to strangers, they are known to be wonderful family dogs. First appeared in 1899 Germany through breeding of farm and herding dogs. They are among the top 5 most itteligent breeds. The most popular color is a mix of beige and black but solid black or white can also be found. Their life expectancy is about 13 years.

Siberian Husky

Siberian Husky, a thickly coated, compact sled dog of medium size and great endurance, was developed to work in packs, pulling light loads at moderate speeds over vast frozen expanses. Sibes are friendly, fastidious, and dignified. A Husky's howl can be heard for 10 miles. A Husky can run 100 miles per day. Huskies saved 10000 lives in name, Alaska. Huskies are one of the oldest breeds in the world. Huskies are great companion and full of energy. The most popular color is black and silver. Their life span expectancy is about 12-15 years. Husky is a general term for a dog used in the polar regions, primarily and specifically for work as sled dogs. It refers to a traditional northern type, notable for its cold-weather tolerance and overall hardiness.Modern racing huskies that maintain arctic breed traits (also known as Alaskan huskies) represent an ever-changing crossbreed of the fastest dogs.

Pomeranian

The Pomeranian is a breed of dog of the Spitz type that is named for the Pomerania region in north-west Poland and north-east Germany in Central Europe. Classed as a toy dog breed because of its small size, the Pomeranian is descended from larger Spitz-type dogs, specifically the German Spitz As mentioned, Pomeranians originated in the province of Pomerania, which is a region of Northern Europe on the coast of the Baltic Sea. They can trace their roots back to large sled dogs and are close relatives to breeds such as the American Eskimo Dog, Alaskan Malamute, Siberian Husky, Norwegian Elkhound, and Samoyed.
